Seedlings

2012
Feb
01

Starting a garden is simple. Grab a pack of seeds. Grab some dirt. Grab some plastic pots. Place dirt in pots. Poke a hole in dirt. Place seed in whole. Cover seed. Wait. Water. Wait. Water. Wait. Water. Keep warm. Eventually you have seedlings.

Once the seedlings are going, they likely still need warmth, and now need some regular light. But don't take them outdoors too quickly. A cold snap, or wind, could wipe out all of your seedlings in the matter of minutes. Not to mention a hungry bird might fancy itself a snack of your toil and effort.

Keeping the seedlings indoors but near a light and warmth source is a good plan. If you don't have a good window, or you just don't want the seedlings indoors, try a greenhouse. Vintage greenhouses are wonderful, but today there are cost and time affordable tent like greenhouses. Easy to setup, easy to take down, no problem.
